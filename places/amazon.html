Amazon Rainforest

The Amazon Rainforest, often called the “Lungs of the Earth,” is the largest tropical rainforest in the world, stretching across nine South American countries. It is home to an extraordinary diversity of plants, animals, and indigenous cultures that make it one of the most fascinating places on Earth.

The mighty Amazon River flows through this lush jungle, offering visitors breathtaking boat journeys, opportunities to spot exotic wildlife such as pink dolphins, jaguars, and colorful macaws, and a chance to experience life in remote rainforest lodges.

Best Time to Visit

June to September, when the weather is drier and wildlife viewing is at its best.

Things to Do

  • Take a river cruise along the Amazon River.
  • Visit indigenous communities and learn about their traditions.
  • Go on jungle treks to spot wildlife and rare plants.
  • Explore Manaus, the gateway city to the Amazon.
