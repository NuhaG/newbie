Aurora Borealis

The Aurora Borealis, or Northern Lights, is one of nature’s most magical displays. Dancing waves of green, purple, and pink light illuminate the polar skies, creating an unforgettable spectacle. This natural phenomenon is caused by solar particles colliding with Earth’s magnetic field.

The best places to see the aurora are in Arctic regions such as Norway, Iceland, Canada, and Finland, where the dark winter nights provide a perfect canvas for this breathtaking show.

Best Time to Visit

September to March, when nights are longest and skies are darkest.

Things to Do

  • Join a guided aurora hunting tour.
  • Stay in a glass igloo in Lapland.
  • Go dog sledding or snowmobiling under the lights.
  • Relax in hot springs while watching the night sky.
