Colosseum

The Colosseum in Rome, Italy, is the largest ancient amphitheater ever built and one of the greatest works of Roman architecture. Constructed in 70–80 AD, it once hosted gladiator battles, animal hunts, and public spectacles.

Today, it stands as a symbol of the Roman Empire’s grandeur and is one of the most visited monuments in the world, attracting millions of travelers who wish to step back into ancient history.

Best Time to Visit

April to June and September to October, with mild weather and fewer crowds.

Things to Do

  • Take a guided tour of the Colosseum’s underground.
  • Explore the Roman Forum nearby.
  • Visit Palatine Hill for sweeping views of ancient Rome.
  • Enjoy Italian cuisine in the surrounding area.
